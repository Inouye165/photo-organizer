- Today:
  - Photo Organizer scans real folders, indexes real photos into the database, generates browse-ready variants, and lets you review the library by date.
  - The current product focus is reliable ingestion, clear scan outcomes, and a calm browsing surface for real photo collections.

- Phase 1 includes:
  - real filesystem scanning against configured roots
  - durable indexing of accepted photos and generated variants
  - resilient per-file scan error tracking
  - gallery browsing, date filtering, and focused inspection overlays
  - trustworthy empty, loading, success, and error states backed by real API data

- Real data only means:
  - no mocked gallery items in the product UI
  - no fake counts, placeholder scans, or invented error states
  - every visible photo, count, and failure record must come from the real backend and database

- Quality bar:
  - calm and polished like a serious consumer product
  - clear hierarchy, intentional spacing, and professional copy
  - resilient backend behavior that does not lose useful diagnostic detail
  - predictable, reviewable changes that stay within the app's real scope

- Future direction:
  - metadata-driven search
  - semantic search
  - labeling and training workflows
  - person and animal recognition
